文法

名詞子句 (Noun Clause)

A noun clause acts as a noun and can be used as the subject, object, or complement. It starts with words like that, if, whether, where, when, how, and so on. For example, in this video, the speaker said, “I’m gonna show you how to make ice cream using ingredients you probably already have at home.” In this sentence, “how to make ice cream using ingredients you probably already have at home” is a noun clause. Though it starts with the question word “how”, the speaker wasn’t trying to ask the audience a question but to talk about “the way, the method” that you can make ice cream at home. Let’s look at the other example sentence: “This (salt) is what’s gonna help bring the temperature down.” Here, “what’s gonna help bring the temperature down” is also a noun clause, and it is not a question, either. The speaker was talking about “the thing” that is going to bring the temperature down.

「名詞子句」是指能扮演名詞角色的子句，可以做為主詞、補語、受詞使用，由 that, if, whether, where, when, how 等等開頭，比如影片中講者說 I’m gonna show you how to make ice cream using ingredients you probably already have at home.「我要教你如何用家裡可能已經有的材料來製作冰淇淋。」此句中 how to make ice cream using ingredients you probably already have at home 為名詞子句，雖然以疑問詞 how 開頭，講者並不是在問觀眾問題，而是要講解你可以在家自己做冰淇淋的「方法」。 我們來看另一個例子：This (salt) is what’s gonna help bring the temperature down.「這（鹽巴）是用來幫忙降溫的。」這裡 what’s gonna help bring the temperature down 也是名詞子句，一樣不是問句，講者是在說明可以幫忙降溫的「事物」。
